Mediation in Belarus


Mediation in Belarus is an alternative way of conflict resolution, which allows the parties to come to a mutually beneficial agreement with the help of a neutral mediator. This method has been actively developing since 2011, when a pilot project was conducted with the support of the Supreme Economic Court of the Republic of Belarus.

In 2014, the Law of the Republic of Belarus "On Mediation" was adopted, which regulates the procedure, requirements for mediators and organizations providing mediation. Mediation can be applied both before going to court and at any stage of the trial. It remains a voluntary and confidential procedure.

Mediation is successfully used to resolve economic, family, labor and corporate disputes. Training courses for mediators are also conducted in Belarus, and the Ministry of Justice maintains a Register of Mediators and organizations providing mediation.3

Development perspective


The prospects for the development of mediation in Belarus look promising. In recent years, there has been an active expansion of the use of mediation in various fields, such as family, labor and economic disputes. The Ministry of Justice is actively working to improve legislation to make mediation more accessible and effective. For example, the Civil and Economic Procedural Codes, as well as the Law on Mediation, have been amended to encourage out-of-court dispute resolution.2

In addition, mediation is becoming an important tool in notary practice and the activities of lawyers, which allows the parties to resolve conflicts without going to court. Business mediation is also developing, which helps companies effectively resolve corporate disputes.3

Future

The development trends of mediation in Belarus show its gradual strengthening as an effective way of dispute resolution. Out-of-court mediation is becoming increasingly popular due to changes in legislation that encourage its use in various fields, such as family, economic and labor disputes. The future of mediation is linked to its integration into notary practice and the activities of lawyers, which allows the parties to resolve conflicts without going to court. Business mediation is also actively developing, which helps companies resolve corporate disputes.

Key trends

  • New laws and regulations governing mediation.
  • The number of mediator training programs is increasing.
  • The introduction of online mediation and the use of digital platforms.
  • Active use not only in civil matters, but also in business, labor disputes and even in criminal proceedings.
  • Support from international organizations.
